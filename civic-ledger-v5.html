Civic decision infrastructure

From static permits to conditional agreements.

Civic Ledger is an AI supported permissioning system that helps cities turn citizen intent into structured, negotiable and auditable agreements.

It supports public authorities, stewards and communities to assess requests, define workable conditions and record each agreement as a reusable precedent.

A note on authority

Civic Ledger supports human decision making. It does not automate final public decisions.

Each step is designed to be visible, reviewable and accountable. Public servants and stewards retain final authority over outputs.

§ 01 · Problem

Cities operate in real time. Permission systems often do not.

Current public permissioning systems are often too rigid, slow and form based to support timely use of underused urban assets.

Many low to medium risk civic uses, from community care to small enterprise and temporary climate response, fall outside existing permit categories. They face delay, rejection or inconsistent handling.

The problem is not lack of demand. It is the lack of an operational workflow to assess context, route requests and generate conditional approvals.

Problem · 01

Rigid categories

Many civic uses do not fit standard permit types, so they are treated as exceptions.

Problem · 02

Unequal access

People with more administrative knowledge can navigate permission systems more easily.

Problem · 03

Slow coordination

Requests often move manually across fragmented departments, creating delay and inconsistency.

§ 02 · Definition

What is Civic Ledger?

Civic Ledger is a civic decision infrastructure for managing requests to use shared and underused urban assets.

It translates natural language requests into structured proposals, checks them against rules and local context, and routes them through the right decision pathway: direct approval, conditional negotiation or human review.

Rather than asking only whether a request should be approved or denied, Civic Ledger asks: under what conditions could this use become possible, safe and accountable?

Function · 01

Make requests legible

Translate everyday language into structured cases.

Function · 02

Move from yes or no to yes, if

Generate conditional agreements that balance rules, context and trade offs.

Function · 03

Build a learning record

Record each case as a precedent for future decisions and workflow updates.

§ 03 · Audience

Who Civic Ledger helps

Civic Ledger supports the people who request, manage and decide how shared urban assets can be used.

For

Residents & community groups

A clearer route to request and use shared space, especially when existing forms or categories do not fit the activity.

For

Stewards & place operators

A structured way to manage requests, define conditions, share responsibilities and record what happens.

For

Public servants

A consistent workflow for routing, reviewing and learning from non standard cases, while keeping decision authority with human actors.

§ 04 · How it works

From request to reusable precedent

Civic Ledger processes each request through a six step protocol, routes it through a proportional pathway, and keeps final authority with human actors.

Six steps
Step 01

Intent

A citizen, steward or organisation signals what they want to do and why.

Step 02

Proposal

The request, written in everyday language, becomes a structured case.

Step 03

Conditions

Rules, location, timing and local context are used to assess risk and route the case.

Step 04

Agreement

Conditions for use are shaped through structured negotiation where needed.

Step 05

Proof

The agreement is recorded as a verifiable and auditable commitment.

Step 06

Learning

Outcomes from real use inform future rules and workflows — feeding the next request.

Proportional routing

Not every request needs the same level of review. Civic Ledger routes cases through proportional pathways depending on risk, complexity and local context.

Pathway · 01

Direct approval

For low risk uses that meet existing rules and conditions.

Risk: Low · Speed: Immediate
Pathway · 02

Conditional negotiation

For non standard or medium risk uses where a workable agreement may be possible.

Risk: Medium · Speed: Iterative
Pathway · 03

Human review

For higher risk cases that need public officials or relevant authorities to assess the request.

Risk: High · Speed: Considered
Authority

Decisions remain with people.

Civic Ledger structures decision making. It interprets requests, checks rules and context, suggests pathways and drafts conditions.

Final authority remains with the relevant human actors and institutions. Public servants, stewards and authorised reviewers can review, adjust or override outputs at key decision points.

Decision sequence
Interpret → Check → Route → Negotiate → Review → Record
§ 05 · Pilots & use cases

Where Civic Ledger is being tested and imagined

Pilots ground the system in real cities. Use cases are speculative scenarios — sketches of where conditional permissioning could go next.

Pilot Now

Underutilised spaces with Incheon City

A six-month proof of concept currently testing the system’s core mechanism through a conversational interface integrating space registry functions and chat-based workflows. Live cases include the temporary activation of parking spaces and community hubs, exploring how natural language requests can be translated into structured proposals and routed through automatic permission, conditional approval and deliberative processes.

Pilot Future

Underutilised spaces with Daegu Metropolitan City

A planned 36-month engagement with Daegu’s Urban Regeneration Bureau and the Suseong Smart City Division, coordinated through Daegu Technopark. Two pilot sites will process real permissioning cases involving 5–10 steward organisations and 50–100 active users, routing requests through direct approval, conditional negotiation and human review pathways.

Use case Future

Migrant-led commons of care

Speculative scenario — not an active pilot.

In a near-future city, a migrant-led collective wants a stable place for language exchange, shared meals and mutual support — not a one-off booking but an ongoing care circle. Civic Ledger helps them describe the request in their own terms, identify suitable spaces across municipal and civic registries, surface the conditions they would need to meet, and form a shared responsibility circle with co-signers. The agreement evolves as the group does.

Read more →
Use case Future

More-than-human permissioning DIY-cities · Melbourne

Speculative scenario — not an active pilot.

In a near-future Melbourne, micro-architecture objects — street furniture, indoor pieces, modular seating — themselves carry permits. Requests can be initiated by people, by stewards, or by sensors. A park’s trees, fitted with IoT and weather data, send out invitations: “rainbow likely in five hours.”

A resident requests permission to deploy folding chairs and a picnic threshold to receive it. Civic Ledger negotiates between the resident, the steward of the chairs, the park’s stewardship circle and the weather event itself, drafting a temporary agreement that lapses when the rainbow fades. The objects become touchpoints for collective imagination of how cities might soften through small interventions.
